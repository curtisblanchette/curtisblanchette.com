The Atlantic

The Atlantic has been a beacon of the American idea for the past 167 years. In 2024, they announced two important milestones: profitability and 1 million subscriptions. At the same time, The Atlantic’s business, product, and technology teams began exploring the adoption of new technologies. Along the way, they approached Metalab to help unpack what responsible AI exploration could look like for the storied publication.

Let's make one thing clear: Atlantic readers expect to read accurate stories written by real journalists. That's why The Atlantic tasked us with creating a safe space to showcase new ways its product and technology teams could use the latest technology to present and distribute stories.

The Vision

Create a safe and responsible framework to explore the future of AI and journalism.

Metalab became an R&D partner for The Atlantic’s product and technology teams, ultimately helping them design Atlantic Labs—an opt-in public testing group to vet and collect feedback on experiments.

Atlantic Labs

A new place to invite subscribers and non-subscribers into the conversation to weigh in on what makes the cut.

For those who are open to the future and find excitement in being part of change—welcome to Atlantic Labs. We helped their team develop a public testing ground to understand how readers would react to new features.

Wanting to protect the core editorial brand and signal the experimental nature of the experience, Labs leans into a technical aesthetic.

Companion Experiment

Intelligently explore over a century’s worth of archived journalism with a single prompt.

The Atlantic archives were an untapped asset. The problem? There was no easy way for people to search through them to find what they were looking for. Enter Companion, the first Labs experiment we helped conceive.  

With a simple prompt, readers can uncover everything the publication has written on a particular topic, from most to least relevant. Knowing the challenge with prompt tools is often understanding what you can ask for, Companion offers a set of suggestions to spark creativity.

AI Strategy

Our technical approach to this challenge: to abstract the role of the LLM from the content of the archive itself.

Instead of having the LLM directly access the archive, we created an intermediary that translates a user prompt into a search of a vector database, similar to a RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) model.

This approach enabled us to use prompt engineering to wrap the prompt with additional context, preventing misuse and giving us finer control over how it's interpreted into a search of the archive and the resulting experience it delivers.

Labs Platform

A technical environment for The Atlantic to endlessly explore, test, and develop AI solutions internally for years to come.

Atlantic Labs is a developer platform that provides their product and technology teams with the tools they need to experiment and release advanced digital features to enhance the reader experience and potentially inspire The Atlantic's main product stream.

We helped design and develop the platform so that it’s lightweight and technology-agnostic, ready to power future engagements with technologies that may not be known yet. The developer experience enables rapid exploration and publishing so that their team can learn quickly, iterate often, and include readers in discovering new ways of engaging with our authors and content.

Innovation Roadmap

A continuous learning loop to inform and fuel future experiments into perpetuity.

The Labs framework creates a low-risk innovation roadmap, allowing The Atlantic's product and technology teams to run experiments, gather feedback, and learn from insights for years to come.
